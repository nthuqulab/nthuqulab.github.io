ABOUT OUR LAB

What we are doing

We study the latest quantum computing technologies — analyzing, improving, and applying them.

Quantum computing uses the principles of quantum mechanics—like superposition and entanglement—to process information with qubits that can represent multiple states at once. This allows it to tackle certain problems, such as factoring or molecular simulation, much faster than classical computers. Although current quantum machines are still limited by noise and scalability, ongoing research aims to build error-corrected systems capable of achieving true quantum advantage.

Quantum Complexity

We study the complexity of state-of-the-art quantum systems, analyzing how algorithms can be solved under current resources such as higher error rate or restricted depth. Understanding computational complexity helps us identify which problems can benefit most from quantum acceleration.

Optimization

Our research focuses on optimizing quantum algorithms and circuits to reduce error rates, improve efficiency, and minimize resource usage. This involves both theoretical methods and practical implementation strategies to make quantum solutions more reliable and scalable.

Random Unitary

We explore pseudorandom unitary operations, essential for simulating complex quantum dynamics and benchmarking devices. These operations help test randomness, entanglement, and information scrambling in quantum systems.

Randomness Device Benchmarks

Cryptography

We investigate quantum cryptography and secure communication protocols, such as quantum key distribution and post-quantum encryption. Our goal is to ensure data security in a future where quantum computers can break classical cryptosystems.
